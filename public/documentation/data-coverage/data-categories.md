# Data Categories

> Understanding how Perception classifies and organizes data

## Overview

Every data point in Perception is enriched with multiple classification layers, making it easy to filter, analyze, and understand media coverage patterns.

## Sentiment Classification

Every data point is classified by tone:

### Positive
Coverage with favorable tone, optimistic outlook, or supportive framing.

**Examples:**
- "Bitcoin Surges to New All-Time High"
- "Institutional Adoption Accelerates"
- "Major Bank Launches Bitcoin Services"

### Neutral
Factual reporting without strong positive or negative framing.

**Examples:**
- "Bitcoin Trades at $95,000"
- "SEC Receives New Bitcoin ETF Application"
- "Company Announces Bitcoin Treasury Strategy"

### Negative
Coverage with critical tone, pessimistic outlook, or concerning framing.

**Examples:**
- "Bitcoin Crashes 20% in Single Day"
- "Exchange Faces Regulatory Scrutiny"
- "Security Breach Affects Thousands"

## Outlet Categories

Media sources are classified by type:

| Category | Description | Examples |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Mainstream Media** | Traditional news outlets | Reuters, Bloomberg, BBC |
| **Crypto Native** | Cryptocurrency-focused publications | CoinDesk, Cointelegraph |
| **Financial Media** | Business and investment publications | MarketWatch, Yahoo Finance |
| **Social Media** | Social platforms and community | X/Twitter, Reddit |

## Trend Categories

Trends are tagged by narrative type:

### Price Movement
Market price changes, trading activity, and volatility.

**Examples:**
- "Bitcoin Breaks $100K Resistance Level"
- "Crypto Market Sees $2B Liquidations"

### Regulatory Development
Legal, policy, and government actions affecting Bitcoin.

**Examples:**
- "SEC Approves First Spot Bitcoin ETF"
- "EU Finalizes MiCA Cryptocurrency Regulations"

### Capital Flow
Investment, institutional activity, and treasury movements.

**Examples:**
- "BlackRock Bitcoin ETF Sees Record Inflows"
- "MicroStrategy Acquires Additional 5,000 BTC"

### Competitive Move
Company actions, partnerships, and business developments.

**Examples:**
- "Tesla Resumes Bitcoin Payments"
- "PayPal Launches Bitcoin Trading in UK"

### Market Sentiment
Trader behavior, market psychology, and sentiment shifts.

**Examples:**
- "Fear & Greed Index Reaches Extreme Greed"
- "Bitcoin Long Positions Hit Record High"

### National Strategy
Government and nation-level Bitcoin decisions.

**Examples:**
- "El Salvador Adds to National Bitcoin Reserve"
- "Germany Completes Bitcoin Divestiture"

## Using Categories for Analysis

### Filter Strategies

**For PR monitoring:**
- Filter by your company + all sentiment types
- Compare positive vs negative ratio over time

**For market research:**
- Filter by trend category (e.g., "Regulatory Development")
- Track sentiment by category

**For competitive analysis:**
- Compare outlet coverage across competitors
- Track which outlet categories cover whom

### Combining Filters

You can combine multiple filters:
- Sentiment + Date Range
- Outlet Category + Topic
- Sentiment + Outlet
