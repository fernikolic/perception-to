# Weekly Reports

> Automated intelligence summaries delivered to your inbox

## Overview

Weekly Reports provide a comprehensive summary of all your watchlist activity over the past week. Each report includes quantitative metrics, sentiment analysis, outlet breakdowns, and automatically generated professional analysis.

## What's in a Weekly Report

### For Each Watchlist Item

| Section | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Mention Count** | Total data points matching your topic this week |
| **Week-over-Week Change** | Percentage increase or decrease vs last week |
| **Sentiment Breakdown** | Positive, neutral, and negative percentages |
| **Top 5 Outlets** | Which sources covered your topic most |
| **Analysis** | Professional-quality narrative analysis |

### Example Report Content

```
Treasuries
This Week: 287 mentions (↓ 8%)
Last Week: 312 mentions

Sentiment:
- 35% Positive
- 51% Neutral
- 14% Negative

Top Outlets:
1. Financial Times - 45 mentions
2. Bloomberg - 38 mentions
3. Reuters - 32 mentions
4. CoinDesk - 28 mentions
5. The Block - 22 mentions

Analysis:
Treasury coverage declined 8% this week, reflecting reduced
market focus following the Fed's unchanged rate decision.
Coverage sentiment remained largely neutral with financial
media dominating the narrative around institutional positioning.
```

## Configuring Weekly Reports

### Enable/Disable
Toggle weekly reports on or off in **Settings > Notifications**.

### Delivery Schedule

| Setting | Options |
|---------|---------|
| **Day** | Sunday, Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday |
| **Time** | 12am, 1am, 2am... through 11pm (24 options) |

**Default:** Monday at 9am in your local timezone

### Timezone

Choose from 70+ timezones organized by region:
- **UTC**
- **North America**: New York, Chicago, Denver, Los Angeles, etc.
- **South America**: São Paulo, Buenos Aires, Lima, etc.
- **Europe**: London, Paris, Berlin, Rome, etc.
- **Middle East & Africa**: Dubai, Jerusalem, Istanbul, etc.
- **Asia**: Tokyo, Singapore, Hong Kong, Mumbai, etc.
- **Australia & Pacific**: Sydney, Melbourne, Auckland, etc.

### Minimum Mentions Threshold

Only receive reports when your watchlist items have meaningful coverage:
- Set to **0** for reports regardless of activity
- Set to **10+** to skip quiet weeks

## Viewing Past Reports

### Report History Archive

Access your last 20 weekly reports directly in the app:

1. Go to **Settings > Notifications**
2. Click **View Reports**
3. See a list of past reports with:
   - Date range covered
   - When the report was sent
   - Number of watchlist items tracked
   - Preview of metrics for each item
4. Click **View Report** to see the full email content

## Automated Analysis

Each watchlist item includes professional-quality analysis generated by our proprietary systems.

### What the Analysis Covers

- **Volume trends**: Is coverage increasing or decreasing?
- **Sentiment shifts**: Any notable changes in tone?
- **Outlet patterns**: Who's driving the narrative?
- **Context**: What does this mean for your topic?

### Analysis Quality

The analysis is written like a senior research analyst at a top consulting firm would write:
- Data-driven with specific numbers
- Actionable with strategic implications
- Forward-looking with trend observations
- Professional in tone and vocabulary

### Example Analysis

> "Coverage of Bitcoin ETFs increased 23% this week following
> BlackRock's amended S-1 filing. Financial media outlets led
> the narrative with notably positive sentiment (42%),
> suggesting growing institutional confidence. Organizations
> monitoring this space should prepare for potential regulatory
> decisions in the coming weeks."

---

## Tips for Getting Value from Reports

1. **Review at a consistent time**: Build a habit around report delivery
2. **Start with the change indicators**: Focus on significant increases or decreases
3. **Read the analysis**: It synthesizes what the numbers mean
4. **Check sentiment shifts**: A sudden change often signals something important
5. **Note new outlets**: When different sources start covering your topic, narratives may be shifting

---

## When Reports Don't Send

Reports won't be sent if:
- You've disabled weekly reports in Settings
- It's not your scheduled delivery day/time
- You have no watchlist items
- A report was already sent today (duplicate prevention)
