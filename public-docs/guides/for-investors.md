# Guide for Investors & VCs

> Monitor portfolio companies, track market sentiment, and identify opportunities

## Overview

Investors need reliable market intelligence to monitor portfolio companies, evaluate new opportunities, and understand market sentiment. Perception provides comprehensive media monitoring with trend analysis to support investment decisions.

## Your Key Use Cases

### 1. Portfolio Monitoring

**Goal:** Track coverage and sentiment for companies in your portfolio.

**How to do it:**
1. Create watchlist items for each portfolio company
2. Set up weekly reports for regular summaries
3. Enable real-time alerts for significant coverage
4. Generate quarterly briefs for LP reporting

**What to monitor:**
- Coverage volume changes (attention increasing/decreasing)
- Sentiment shifts (early warning of issues)
- Competitive coverage comparison
- Key narrative evolution

### 2. Due Diligence

**Goal:** Research potential investments with comprehensive media analysis.

**How to do it:**
1. Search for the target company
2. Analyze historical coverage patterns
3. Review sentiment trends over time
4. Generate an Investor Relations brief

**Key questions to answer:**
- How is the company perceived in media?
- Any red flags in coverage history?
- How do they compare to competitors?
- What's the market narrative?

### 3. Market Sentiment Tracking

**Goal:** Understand overall market mood and direction.

**How to do it:**
1. Create watchlist items for market-level keywords
2. Track the Trends page for narrative shifts
3. Monitor sentiment distribution over time
4. Compare current sentiment to historical patterns

**Keywords to track:**
- "Bitcoin bull market" / "Bitcoin bear market"
- "Crypto winter" / "Crypto adoption"
- "Institutional Bitcoin"
- Regulatory themes

### 4. Thesis Validation

**Goal:** Test investment theses with coverage data.

**How to do it:**
1. Create watchlist items for thesis keywords
2. Track coverage volume trends
3. Monitor sentiment evolution
4. Compare to competing thesis narratives

## Best Practices

### Portfolio Watchlist Setup

For each portfolio company, include:
```
Name: [Company Name]
Search terms: "[Company], [Ticker], [CEO name], [Product names]"
```

**Tip:** Create a separate "sector" watchlist item to track industry-level coverage.

### Using Weekly Reports

Weekly reports are ideal for investor monitoring:
- Quantitative metrics for tracking
- Week-over-week comparisons
- Automated analysis for quick synthesis
- Historical archive for trend analysis

### Interpreting for Investment Decisions

| Signal | Possible Meaning | Action |
|--------|------------------|--------|
| Coverage spike + positive sentiment | Good news breaking | Monitor for opportunity |
| Coverage spike + negative sentiment | Crisis or challenge | Dig deeper immediately |
| Declining coverage | Losing relevance | Evaluate position |
| Sentiment shifting negative | Market concern | Investigate root cause |

### Weekly Portfolio Review

**Time needed:** 15-20 minutes

1. Review weekly reports for all portfolio companies
2. Note any significant coverage changes
3. Check sentiment trends
4. Flag items requiring deeper analysis
5. Update investment committee notes

### Quarterly LP Reporting

**Time needed:** 1 hour per company

1. Generate "Investor Relations" briefs for each portfolio company
2. Review 90-day coverage trends
3. Compile sentiment and volume data
4. Summarize market narrative for LP communications

### New Investment Research

**Time needed:** 30 minutes

1. Search for target company (full date range)
2. Review historical coverage patterns
3. Generate IR brief for comprehensive analysis
4. Compare to sector and competitor coverage
5. Note any coverage concerns for diligence

## Generating Investor Briefs

For investment-focused analysis:

1. Select the company or market theme
2. Choose **"Investor Relations"** audience
3. Review the brief for:
   - Investment thesis clarity
   - Financial media coverage analysis
   - Market perception assessment
   - Valuation impact considerations

### What IR Briefs Include

- **Investment Thesis**: How the market views the investment case
- **Media Perception**: Coverage quality and sentiment
- **Analyst Coverage**: Who's covering and how
- **Risk Factors**: Concerns appearing in coverage
- **Recommendations**: Investment communication actions

## Advanced Strategies

### Early Warning System

Set up alerts to catch portfolio issues early:
1. Enable real-time alerts for all portfolio companies
2. Set threshold to "All" for comprehensive coverage
3. Watch for sudden sentiment shifts
4. Monitor for coverage spikes

### Thesis Tracking Over Time

1. Create watchlist items for thesis keywords
2. Save weekly report data monthly
3. Build a time-series view of thesis momentum
4. Use trends to identify thesis inflection points

### Sector Analysis

1. Create broad sector watchlist items
2. Track multiple competitors simultaneously
3. Compare relative coverage and sentiment
4. Identify market share of voice leaders

### Exit Timing Intelligence

When considering exit:
1. Track coverage momentum (selling into strength)
2. Monitor sentiment trajectory
3. Watch competitor coverage (market context)
4. Note any emerging negative narratives

## Example: Portfolio Monitoring Setup

**Scenario:** You have 5 Bitcoin-related companies in your portfolio.

**Setup:**

1. **Create watchlist items:**
   - Company A (with all name variants)
   - Company B (with all name variants)
   - Company C (with all name variants)
   - Company D (with all name variants)
   - Company E (with all name variants)
   - "Bitcoin [Your Sector]" (sector-level)

2. **Configure alerts:**
   - Real-time: High impact only (reduce noise)
   - Weekly digest: Every Monday 8am

3. **Weekly routine:**
   - Monday: Review all weekly reports (15 min)
   - Daily: Quick scan of Trends for sector news
   - As needed: Generate briefs for deep dives

4. **Quarterly:**
   - Generate IR briefs for all companies
   - Compare coverage trends
   - Prepare LP communication materials
