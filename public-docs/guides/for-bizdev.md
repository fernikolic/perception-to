# Guide for Business Development Teams

> Identify opportunities, track partners, and monitor the competitive landscape

## Overview

Business development professionals need market intelligence to identify partnership opportunities, understand competitive positioning, and spot emerging market signals. Perception provides the coverage data and trend analysis to inform your BD strategy.

## Your Key Use Cases

### 1. Partnership Opportunity Identification

**Goal:** Find potential partners and track their market activity.

**How to do it:**
1. Create watchlist items for potential partner companies
2. Track their media coverage and announcements
3. Monitor sentiment to gauge market perception
4. Watch for funding, expansion, or strategic signals

**What signals a good partnership opportunity:**
- Positive press momentum
- Strategic announcements (new markets, products)
- Funding rounds or financial strength signals
- Complementary coverage themes

### 2. Competitive Intelligence

**Goal:** Understand competitor positioning and activity.

**How to do it:**
1. Create watchlist items for each major competitor
2. Compare coverage volume and sentiment weekly
3. Track their messaging and narratives
4. Identify their media relationships

**What to track:**
- New product or feature announcements
- Partnership announcements
- Hiring signals (expansion or contraction)
- Regulatory issues or challenges

### 3. Market Expansion Signals

**Goal:** Identify new market opportunities.

**How to do it:**
1. Monitor regional coverage patterns
2. Track emerging company categories
3. Watch regulatory developments by geography
4. Follow industry trend narratives

### 4. Deal Intelligence

**Goal:** Support active BD conversations with market data.

**How to do it:**
1. Generate briefs for companies you're in discussions with
2. Track their recent coverage and sentiment
3. Understand their market narrative
4. Identify potential objections or concerns from coverage

## Workflow Examples

### Weekly BD Intelligence Routine

**Monday Morning:**
1. Review weekly reports for all tracked companies
2. Identify any significant coverage changes
3. Note companies with positive momentum

**Throughout Week:**
1. Check Trends daily for new opportunities
2. Monitor active prospect coverage
3. Flag competitive announcements

**Friday:**
1. Compile intelligence for leadership
2. Update prospect priority based on signals
3. Identify next week's outreach targets

### Pre-Meeting Research

**1 hour before meeting:**
1. Search for company name (last 7 days)
2. Review sentiment and top stories
3. Generate a quick brief (Business Development audience)
4. Note 2-3 conversation starters from coverage

## Generating BD Briefs

When preparing for strategic conversations:

1. Select the target company or market
2. Choose **"Business Development"** audience
3. Review the brief for:
   - Partnership readiness signals
   - Market positioning insights
   - Competitive landscape context
   - Opportunity assessment

### What BD Briefs Include

- **Market Position**: How the target is perceived
- **Partnership Signals**: Indicators of openness to deals
- **Competitive Context**: Where they fit in the landscape
- **Opportunity Assessment**: Specific BD recommendations
- **Conversation Starters**: Topics to discuss based on coverage

## Advanced Strategies

### Building a Partnership Pipeline

1. **Tier companies** by coverage momentum and sentiment
2. **Prioritize** those with positive trajectories
3. **De-prioritize** those facing public challenges (unless you can help)
4. **Track timing** - reach out when news is positive

### Competitive Positioning

1. **Track competitor partnerships** via their coverage
2. **Identify gaps** they're not filling
3. **Monitor their challenges** for opportunity angles
4. **Note their messaging** to differentiate

### Market Timing

1. **Watch regulatory trends** - compliance needs drive partnerships
2. **Track funding announcements** - well-funded companies make better partners
3. **Monitor market sentiment** - bullish markets mean more deal activity
4. **Follow category trends** - growing categories have more opportunity

**Scenario:** You're looking for companies to integrate with your payment solution.

**Step 1: Monitor the Category**
- Create watchlist: "Bitcoin payments, BTC payment, crypto payment processor"
- Review weekly for active companies

**Step 2: Identify Candidates**
- Note companies appearing frequently in coverage
- Check their sentiment (positive = good prospect)
- Look for growth signals

**Step 3: Research Specific Targets**
- Create watchlist items for top candidates
- Generate BD briefs for each
- Track for 2-3 weeks before outreach

**Step 4: Time Your Outreach**
- Reach out after positive news (funding, partnership)
- Avoid outreach during crisis coverage
- Reference specific coverage in your approach
