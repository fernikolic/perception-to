# Guide for Executives

> High-level market intelligence for strategic decision-making

## Overview

Executives need concise, actionable intelligence without drowning in details. Perception delivers synthesized market intelligence through automatically generated briefs, automated reports, and trend alerts that surface what matters.

---

## Your Key Use Cases

### 1. Market Awareness

**Goal:** Stay informed on Bitcoin market dynamics without manual research.

**How to do it:**
1. Set up a watchlist with key topics (your company, competitors, market themes)
2. Enable weekly digest emails
3. Check the Trends page when you have 5 minutes
4. Generate briefs when you need deeper context

**Time commitment:** 10-15 minutes per week

### 2. Competitive Intelligence

**Goal:** Understand how competitors are positioned in the market.

**How to do it:**
1. Create watchlist items for key competitors
2. Review weekly reports comparing coverage
3. Generate executive briefs before strategic meetings
4. Note sentiment differences vs your organization

### 3. Board Preparation

**Goal:** Have data-backed market context for board discussions.

**How to do it:**
1. Generate "C-Suite Executive" briefs for relevant topics
2. Review 30-day coverage trends
3. Note key narratives and sentiment patterns
4. Extract strategic implications

### 4. Crisis Awareness

**Goal:** Get early warning of market or reputational issues.

**How to do it:**
1. Enable real-time alerts for your company
2. Set threshold to "High" to avoid noise
3. Monitor for coverage spikes or sentiment shifts
4. Check Trends for emerging negative narratives

The "C-Suite Executive" brief is designed for your needs:

### What You Get

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Executive Summary** | Key findings in 2-3 sentences |
| **Strategic Implications** | What this means for business |
| **Market Position** | How you're perceived vs competitors |
| **Risk Assessment** | Potential concerns to monitor |
| **Recommendations** | Suggested actions |

### When to Generate Briefs

- Before board meetings
- Before earnings calls
- During market volatility
- When competitors make news
- For strategic planning sessions

### How to Use Briefs

1. Read the executive summary first
2. Scan strategic implications
3. Note any risk flags
4. Use recommendations for discussion points
5. Share with leadership team as needed

## Key Metrics to Watch

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Week-over-Week Change** | Is attention growing or declining? |
| **Sentiment Direction** | Market perception trajectory |
| **Coverage vs Competitors** | Relative market mindshare |
| **Signal Strength Trends** | Which narratives are gaining traction |

### Warning Signs

Watch for these signals in your weekly reports:

| Signal | Concern Level | Action |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Sentiment shift (positive → negative) | High | Investigate immediately |
| Coverage spike (3x+ normal) | Medium | Understand what's driving it |
| Competitor coverage surge | Low | Monitor for competitive threat |
| New negative narrative | High | Prepare response strategy |

## Workflow Examples

## Delegating Intelligence Work

### What to Delegate

- Setting up detailed watchlist items
- Configuring alert preferences
- Running regular searches
- Compiling weekly summaries

### What to Review Personally

- Weekly digest emails (curated for you)
- Executive briefs before key meetings
- Trends during market volatility
- Crisis-related alerts

### How to Delegate Effectively

1. Have an assistant set up your watchlist
2. Request they generate briefs before key meetings
3. Ask for a weekly 1-paragraph summary of highlights
4. Maintain real-time alerts to your email for emergencies

## Tips for Maximum Value, Minimum Time

1. **Trust the weekly digest**: It's designed to surface what matters
2. **Use briefs strategically**: Generate only when you need depth
3. **Focus on changes**: Week-over-week shifts matter more than absolutes
4. **Set appropriate alert thresholds**: "High" only reduces noise significantly
5. **Check Trends during volatility**: Best early warning system

## Common Questions

### "I don't have time to check another platform"

That's why we built weekly digests. The email comes to you with:
- Automated analysis
- Key metrics at a glance
- Action items highlighted

You don't need to log in unless you want more detail.

### "How is this different from just reading the news?"

Perception aggregates 50+ sources, analyzes sentiment, identifies patterns, and synthesizes findings. You get the conclusions without reading hundreds of articles.

### "What if I miss something important?"

Real-time alerts notify you within 30 minutes of significant coverage. Set threshold to "High" to only receive alerts that matter.
