# Key Concepts

> Understand the terminology and concepts used throughout Perception

---

## Core Concepts

### Data Points
Individual pieces of content that Perception monitors and analyzes. This includes news articles, social posts, press releases, and other media content. Perception tracks 560,000+ data points and growing.

### Watchlist
Your personalized list of topics, companies, or keywords that you want to track. Each watchlist item tells Perception what to monitor and surface for you.

### Search Terms
The keywords and phrases associated with a watchlist item. Use multiple terms separated by commas to capture variations (e.g., "MicroStrategy, MSTR, Saylor Bitcoin").

---

## Sentiment Analysis

### Sentiment Score
Every data point is classified as:
- **Positive**: Favorable coverage, optimistic tone
- **Neutral**: Factual reporting, balanced coverage
- **Negative**: Critical coverage, pessimistic tone

### Sentiment Distribution
The breakdown of positive, neutral, and negative coverage over a time period. Useful for understanding overall media tone.

---

## Trends System

### Trend
An emerging narrative or story pattern identified by our AI. Trends are specific and actionable (e.g., "MicroStrategy Acquires Additional 5,000 BTC" rather than generic "Bitcoin News").

### Signal Strength
How established a trend is based on coverage volume:
- **Early**: 1-2 sources reporting (breaking story)
- **Emerging**: 3-5 sources (growing story)
- **Strong**: 6+ sources (established narrative)

### Categories
Trends are tagged by type:
- **Price Movement**: Market price changes
- **Regulatory Development**: Legal and policy news
- **Capital Flow**: Investment and institutional activity
- **Competitive Move**: Company actions and announcements
- **Market Sentiment**: Trader behavior and mood
- **National Strategy**: Government-level decisions

---

## Media Sources

### Outlet
A publication or media source (e.g., CoinDesk, Bloomberg, Reuters).

### Outlet Category
How we classify media sources:
- **Mainstream Media**: Traditional news outlets
- **Crypto Native**: Bitcoin and crypto-focused publications
- **Financial Media**: Business and finance publications
- **Social Media**: X/Twitter, Reddit, etc.

---

## Reports & Briefs

### Quick Brief
An AI-generated intelligence report that analyzes coverage for a specific topic. Customized based on your selected audience (PR, Executive, etc.).

### Weekly Report
An automated email summary of your watchlist activity over the past week. Includes:
- Mention volume and week-over-week changes
- Sentiment breakdown
- Top outlets covering your topics
- AI-powered professional analysis

### Report Audience
Who the report is written for. This affects the tone, focus, and recommendations:
- **C-Suite Executive**: Strategic implications and market positioning
- **PR & Communications**: Media relationships and narrative control
- **Business Development**: Partnership opportunities and market signals
- **Investor Relations**: Investment narrative and analyst coverage
- **Marketing Team**: Content strategy and audience engagement
- **Product Team**: User sentiment and feature feedback

---

## Search & Filtering

### Hybrid Search
Perception's search system that combines keyword matching with semantic understanding. Search across all 560,000+ data points instantly.

### Saved Searches
Store frequently used search queries for quick access. Each saved search preserves your keywords and filters.

### Date Range
Filter data points by publication date. You can view:
- Last 24 hours
- Last 7 days
- Last 30 days
- Custom date range

---

## Alerts & Notifications

### Real-Time Alerts
Notifications sent within 30 minutes when important coverage matches your watchlist. Filtered by urgency:
- **Breaking News**: Major developing stories
- **High Impact**: Significant coverage from major outlets
- **Regulatory Update**: Policy and legal developments

### Weekly Digest
A comprehensive email summary sent on your chosen day and time. Includes all watchlist items with analysis.

### Alert Threshold
Filter which alerts you receive:
- **All**: Every matching data point
- **Medium**: Only notable coverage
- **High**: Only significant developments

---

## Metrics

### Mention Count
The number of data points that match a search term over a time period.

### Week-over-Week Change
Percentage change in mention count compared to the previous week. Useful for identifying growing or declining coverage.

### Top Outlets
The media sources with the most coverage of a topic during a time period.
