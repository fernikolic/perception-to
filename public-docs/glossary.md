# Glossary

> Definitions of terms used in Perception

---

## A

### Alert
A notification sent when coverage matching your watchlist meets certain criteria. Can be real-time (within 30 minutes) or weekly digest format.

### Alert Threshold
The significance level required to trigger a real-time alert. Options: All, Medium, High.

---

## B

### Brief
See [Quick Brief](#quick-brief).

---

## C

### Categories (Trend)
Classification tags for trends indicating the type of narrative: Price Movement, Regulatory Development, Capital Flow, Competitive Move, Market Sentiment, National Strategy.

---

## D

### Data Point
An individual piece of content tracked by Perception, such as a news article, social post, or press release.

### Digest
See [Weekly Digest](#weekly-digest).

---

## E

### Early (Signal Strength)
A trend signal strength indicating 1-2 sources are reporting. Represents a breaking story with higher uncertainty.

### Emerging (Signal Strength)
A trend signal strength indicating 3-5 sources are reporting. Represents a growing story with moderate confidence.

---

## I

### Intelligence Feed
The main data view in Perception showing all relevant coverage for your watchlist, filterable by date, sentiment, and source.

---

## M

### Mention Count
The number of data points that match a search term or watchlist item over a time period.

---

## N

### Negative (Sentiment)
Sentiment classification indicating critical tone, pessimistic outlook, or concerning framing in coverage.

### Neutral (Sentiment)
Sentiment classification indicating factual reporting without strong positive or negative framing.

---

## O

### Outlet
A media publication or source (e.g., CoinDesk, Bloomberg, Reuters).

### Outlet Category
Classification of media sources: Mainstream Media, Crypto Native, Financial Media, Social Media.

---

## P

### Positive (Sentiment)
Sentiment classification indicating favorable tone, optimistic outlook, or supportive framing in coverage.

---

## Q

### Quick Brief
An AI-generated intelligence report that analyzes coverage for a specific topic and presents findings tailored to a selected audience type.

---

## R

### Real-Time Alert
An immediate notification sent within 30 minutes when significant coverage matches your watchlist.

---

## S

### Saved Search
A stored search query that can be quickly accessed and re-run from the search interface.

### Search Terms
Keywords and phrases associated with a watchlist item, used to match relevant coverage.

### Sentiment
AI-classified tone of coverage: Positive, Neutral, or Negative.

### Sentiment Distribution
The percentage breakdown of positive, neutral, and negative coverage over a time period.

### Signal Strength
Classification of how established a trend is based on coverage volume: Early, Emerging, or Strong.

### Strong (Signal Strength)
A trend signal strength indicating 6+ sources are reporting. Represents an established narrative with high confidence.

---

## T

### Trend
An emerging narrative or story pattern identified by Perception's AI. Trends are specific and actionable rather than generic topics.

---

## W

### Watchlist
Your personalized collection of topics, companies, or keywords that you want to monitor.

### Watchlist Item
A single entry in your watchlist, consisting of a name, search terms, and optional settings.

### Week-over-Week Change
The percentage change in mention count compared to the previous week, used to identify growing or declining coverage.

### Weekly Digest
A comprehensive email summary sent on a scheduled day/time containing analysis of all watchlist activity over the past week.
