# Frequently Asked Questions

> Quick answers to common questions

## Getting Started

### What is Perception?
Perception is a Bitcoin market intelligence platform that aggregates and analyzes media coverage from 50+ sources. It helps you track topics, discover trends, and receive AI-powered intelligence reports.

### How do I start using Perception?
1. Create an account
2. Add topics to your watchlist
3. Configure your notification preferences
4. Explore the Intelligence Feed and Trends pages

See the [Quick Start Guide](./getting-started/quick-start.md) for detailed steps.

### Is there a mobile app?
Perception is a web application optimized for desktop and mobile browsers. There is no native mobile app at this time.

## Data & Coverage

### How much data does Perception have?
Perception tracks 560,000+ data points from 50+ media outlets, with coverage spanning from 2011 to present. New data points are added continuously.

### What sources do you monitor?
We monitor mainstream media (Reuters, Bloomberg, Forbes), crypto-native publications (CoinDesk, Cointelegraph), financial media (MarketWatch, Yahoo Finance), and social platforms. See [Sources Overview](./data-coverage/sources-overview.md) for details.

### How often is data updated?
- News sources: Multiple times daily
- Trends: Every hour
- Search results: Real-time
- Alerts: Every 30 minutes

See [Update Frequency](./data-coverage/update-frequency.md) for details.

### How accurate is sentiment analysis?
Our AI sentiment classification is trained on cryptocurrency media and achieves high accuracy for clear positive/negative/neutral content. Edge cases with mixed sentiment may vary.

## Features

### What is a watchlist?
Your watchlist is a collection of topics, companies, or keywords you want to track. Perception monitors coverage matching your watchlist items and surfaces relevant content.

### How do Trends work?
Our AI analyzes thousands of data points every hour to identify emerging narratives. Trends are specific, actionable story patterns rather than generic topics. See [Trends Analysis](./features/trends.md).

### What are Quick Briefs?
Quick Briefs are AI-generated intelligence reports that analyze coverage for a specific topic and present findings for a target audience (PR, Executive, Business Development, etc.). See [Quick Briefs](./features/briefs.md).

### How do alerts work?
Real-time alerts notify you within 30 minutes when significant coverage matches your watchlist. Weekly digests provide comprehensive summaries on your chosen schedule. See [Alerts & Notifications](./features/alerts.md).

## Account & Settings

### How do I change my notification preferences?
Go to **Settings > Notifications** to configure real-time alerts, weekly digests, timezone, and delivery schedule.

### Can I change my timezone?
Yes. Go to **Settings > Notifications** and select from 70+ timezone options organized by region.

### How do I add or remove watchlist items?
Navigate to the **Intelligence** page, click **Add to Watchlist** to add new items, or click on existing items to edit or delete them.

### Can I export my data?
Yes. You can export search results and watchlist data in CSV or JSON format. See [Data Exports](./integrations/exports.md).

## Email & Notifications

### I'm not receiving emails. What should I do?
1. Check your notification settings are enabled
2. Verify you have watchlist items configured
3. Confirm your email address is correct
4. Check your spam/junk folder
5. Add `alerts@perception.to` to your contacts

### How do I stop receiving emails?
Go to **Settings > Notifications** and toggle off the email types you don't want. You can also click "Manage preferences" in any email footer.

### Why did I only receive 5 alerts today?
Real-time alerts are limited to 5 per day to prevent alert fatigue. This resets daily.

### Why is there a gap between alerts?
Alerts for the same keyword have a 2-hour minimum gap to avoid notification overload for ongoing stories.

## Briefs & Reports

### How long does it take to generate a brief?
Brief generation typically takes about 2 minutes as our AI analyzes relevant coverage.

### Why should I choose different audiences for briefs?
Each audience type (PR, Executive, Business Development, etc.) produces a differently focused brief. Choose the audience that will actually read the brief for the most relevant content.

### Where can I see past weekly reports?
Go to **Settings > Notifications** and click **View Reports** to access your last 20 weekly reports.
